Powers

Flexible Arms

Arms can extend, stretch, and bend relatively freely, with some limits as to how far. They can also retract if needed, but typically not done because it looks funny. Fingers on the hands can also be repositioned as needed for advanced grip or gesturing.

Flight

Jet-powered thrusters allow hovering and flying for general purpose, but at subsonic levels. This can also function underwater to a limited degree.

Durability

Torso casing is a soft and flexible but strong material that can withstand strong pressures, provided the internals aren't crushed beyond function or casing integrity being punctured or split. Arm casings are flexible to fit the needs of the arms. Visor screen is resistant to impact and scratches. All other parts are decently durable as robots go, including the head and appendage casings being more solid in contrast to the torso. There is even a considerable degree of water resistance with sealing, even with exhaust and thruster vents having a system to avoid water reaching internal electronics, but enough pressure can bypass this resistance and potentially cause damage.

Self-Repair

Internal non-self-replicating nanite system maintains systems and casings internally and externally as needed through daily wear or the occasional severe damage. The process is not instant and requires working materials to function fully. These materials may be consumed through an intake to be used or stored in specific internal compartments as necessary. This assists with general maintenance outside of areas where full repair services with the proper materials and equipment are readily available.

Advanced Charging

In addition to direct power connection, micro solar receptors scattered across the head can pick up some energy from suns. As well, there is a food energy extraction system that can provide charge with about 99% food-to-energy conversion efficiency in optimal conditions, with high energy density foods such as sweets preferred for this purpose. The intake for this system is hidden somewhere under the head. Multiple high-density core batteries, in addition to nanite-driven expandable secondary batteries, supplement the charge systems, allowing for increased power storage and runtime when needed, designed for longer expeditions where charge opportunities are scarce.
